Music Happy.

This is a website full of diverse voices, all collected by Zhang Hao himself. Our website gathers voices from all over the world, from natural landscapes to urban noise, from animal calls to human communication, covering a diverse world of sounds. Whether you are looking for relaxing natural sounds or want to explore unique audio from different corners of the world, we can meet your needs. We are committed to capturing various sounds in life and bringing you unique and authentic experiences. Whether used for music production, sound design, or pure enjoyment, the sound here will bring you a brand new experience.

Mouse

Rat, one of the twelve zodiac animals, is the first of the Earthly Branches. Mice are small, agile, and versatile, with the strongest courage and most frequent activities during their infancy.

Cattle

Ugly Ox, one of the twelve zodiac animals, ranks second in the Earthly Branch. Cows are loyal to their owners throughout their lives and work hard all their lives.

Tiger

Yin Hu, one of the twelve zodiac animals, ranks third in the Earthly Branch. Tigers are known as the king of beasts, fierce and powerful, with the fiercest and most hurtful nature.

Rabbit

Mao Tu, one of the twelve zodiac animals, ranks fourth in the Earthly Branch. The rabbit has a gentle and lovely appearance, innocent and lively, calm and obedient. The radiance of the jade rabbit moon has not yet faded during the Mao period.

Dragon

Chenlong, the only mythical animal among the twelve zodiac signs, ranks fifth in the Earthly Branch. The dragon is a deified symbol.

Snake

The Snake, one of the twelve zodiac animals, ranks sixth in the Earthly Branch. Snakes are sensitive and agile, and when they are agile, they often hide in the grass.

Horse

Wuma is ranked seventh among the twelve zodiac animals. The head of the six horses and livestock, with a strong body and shiny mane.

Sheep

Sheep ranks eighth among the twelve zodiac animals and belongs to the "Wei" category along with the twelve earthly dominions.

Monkey

Monkey is the ninth animal in the Chinese zodiac, corresponding to the earthly branch "Shen".

Chicken

Youji, one of the twelve zodiac animals, ranks tenth in the Earthly Branch. Ji You Shi began to enter the cage and return to the nest, and stayed up overnight.

Dog

The Year of the Dog refers to one of the twelve zodiac years in China. The source comes from the eleventh ranked animal in the Chinese zodiac - the dog.

Pig

Hai Zhu, one of the twelve zodiac animals, is the twelfth of the Earthly Branch.
